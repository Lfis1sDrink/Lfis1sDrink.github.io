Title: Int’l Student Detained After Asking RMB 660K Bride Price

A Chinese national working in the Netherlands has recently reported that a female doctoral student in a university in Rotterdam was detained by the police after asking for a bride price from her Dutch husband-to-be.

It is reported that the woman, surnamed Zhang, got pregnant and shared the news with her boyfriend who received it with excitement. The two then began planning for the wedding, however, the man disappeared and reported her to the police when she asked for 88,000 euros in bride price.

Due to a significant number of incidents involving women/men marrying locals and then dumping them when they secure citizenship, asking for money before marriage is usually considered one of the signs of marriage fraud in the Netherlands.

Background

Zhang was married before she left China for further studies in the Netherlands, and she had demanded 660,000 yuan as bride price. However, she and her then husband divorced after 4 years due to irreconcilable differences.

She then “fell in love” with a local Dutch man while studying in the Netherlands and quickly became pregnant.

While discussing the terms of marriage with the Dutch man, she demanded 88,000 euros from him as a bride price, citing the amount that her first husband paid to her family.

Due to little understanding of the Chinese culture, the Dutch man thought that Zhang was trying to extort money from him in the name of marriage and better yet, he began thinking that she wanted to marry him only for the nationality.

After failure to convince her otherwise, he reported her to the police for marriage fraud.

Zhang was quickly detained by the police and was later charged with marriage fraud.

During the trial, Zhang was found guilty of immigration related marriage  fraud and was sentenced to six months in jail. She will also be deported after completing her sentence.

Netizens’ reactions were varied, however, many praised her for charging the foreigner the same amount of bride price as Chinese, as many commented that many women in China do not ask for bride price from their foreign husbands.

Others though, joked that she might have thought that she was in Henan. 

“That is Hélán, not Henan.” They quipped.

Note: Hélán is the pinyin for the Netherlands, it sounds like the north-central China’s Henan Province.

Some said it is practically also illegal in China, citing the civil code of the PRC, which clearly states that “Thou shan’t ask for money or property in the name of marriage.”
